1. 🛠️ Introduction to Internal Affairs

IA ensures accountability, fairness, and professionalism.
Upholds server rules, code of conduct, and public trust.
IA members remain impartial and do not take sides.

2. 🧑‍⚖️ Responsibilities & Expectations

  • Review complaints and incident reports.
  • Investigate staff or officer misconduct.
  • Maintain confidentiality and neutrality.
  • Communicate clearly and professionally.
  • Report to IA Command or Oklahoma Leadership.

3. 📁 Case Handling Process

  1. Receive complaint (ticket, bot report, or form).
  2. Log the case using IA Tracker or Database.
  3. Assign case number and investigator.
  4. Notify involved parties.
  5. Collect evidence.
  6. Conduct interviews.
  7. Submit findings to IA Command.
  8. Apply disciplinary recommendation.
  9. File report in IA archive.

4. 🧾 Ticket Review & Evidence Collection

  • Use /transcript or /record (PAWA) in ticket channels.
  • Organize and label all evidence clearly.
  • Screenshots must include timestamps.
  • Verify authenticity of all content.

5. 🧪 Interview & Interrogation Techniques

  • Remain calm and respectful.
  • Use neutral language.
  • Avoid assumptions; ask direct questions.
  • Log interviews or record with PAWA.

6. ⚖️ Determining Outcomes & Writing Reports

Use the IA report template and include:

  • Incident summary
  • Evidence list
  • Findings
  • Conclusion & recommendation

7. 🔧 Tools & Systems Overview

  • PAWA Bot: /record in IA channels
  • Google Docs: Shared report templates
  • IA Tracker Sheet: Log and manage cases
  • Embed Bots (e.g. Discohook): Post notices

8. 🧑‍🏫 Mock Case Training

  • Trainer creates a scenario.
  • Trainees must collect evidence.
  • Conduct mock interviews.
  • Submit final IA report to trainer panel.

9. 🏁 Final Evaluation

  • Written scenario exam
  • Mock case evaluation
  • Conduct review
  • Passing score: 85%

10. 📜 Certification Requirements

  • Complete all 10 steps
  • Show skill in reporting & investigation
  • Pass final review by IA Command
