Mission Statement

Our mission is to ensure the highest standards of professionalism, ethics, and behavior among our personnel. The Internal Affairs Division works to uphold trust through impartial investigations and transparent practices.

Roles and Responsibilities

IA Agent Responsibilities

  • Conduct fair, unbiased investigations
  • Maintain confidentiality and neutrality
  • Document findings accurately and timely
  • Monitor department compliance

IA Supervisor Responsibilities

  • Assign and oversee investigations
  • Review reports and evidence thoroughly
  • Recommend appropriate corrective actions

Investigation Process

  1. Receive and log the complaint
  2. Assign case to an IA agent
  3. Gather all relevant evidence
  4. Conduct interviews and document findings
  5. Supervisor reviews the report
  6. Final decision and archiving

Reporting a Complaint

All complaints must be submitted via the official ticketing system with any available evidence. Every report is treated with confidentiality and respect. Updates will be provided throughout the process.

Types of Misconduct

  • Abuse of power or excessive force
  • Discrimination, harassment, or bias
  • Unethical or corrupt behavior
  • Failure to follow standard procedures

Disciplinary Actions

  • Verbal Warning: Informal correction
  • Written Reprimand: Documented warning
  • Training/Retraining: Policy education
  • Suspension: Temporary removal from duty
  • Demotion: Reduction in rank/responsibility
  • Termination: Removal from the department
