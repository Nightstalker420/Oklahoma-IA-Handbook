Oklahoma RP Internal Affairs Handbook

Welcome to the Official Internal Affairs (IA) Handbook for Oklahoma RP. This comprehensive guide is designed to provide clear policies, procedures, responsibilities, and ethical standards for all Internal Affairs personnel and members of the department under IA oversight. The goal is to ensure fairness, transparency, and accountability within Oklahoma RP.

Mission Statement

The mission of the Internal Affairs Division is to uphold the highest standards of honesty, fairness, and integrity throughout all departmental operations. IA ensures that all personnel adhere to these values and that any misconduct is addressed swiftly and justly to maintain public trust and internal discipline.

Core Values

  • Integrity: Acting with honesty and ethical principles at all times.
  • Accountability: Holding all members responsible for their actions.
  • Impartiality: Conducting all investigations without bias or favoritism.
  • Confidentiality: Protecting sensitive information and respecting privacy.
  • Professionalism: Maintaining respectful and objective conduct throughout investigations.

Roles and Responsibilities

IA Agent Responsibilities

  • Conduct thorough and impartial investigations into allegations of misconduct or policy violations.
  • Maintain strict confidentiality of all case details and evidence.
  • Prepare detailed and accurate reports documenting findings and evidence.
  • Communicate regularly with supervisors and involved parties as appropriate.
  • Stay up-to-date with department policies and legal standards.

IA Supervisor Responsibilities

  • Assign cases and monitor progress of investigations to ensure timely completion.
  • Review investigation reports and findings for accuracy and completeness.
  • Recommend appropriate disciplinary or corrective actions based on evidence.
  • Provide guidance and mentorship to IA agents to uphold standards.
  • Coordinate with department leadership to communicate outcomes and policy updates.

Investigation Process

  1. Complaint Receipt: All complaints or reports of misconduct are received through official channels and logged promptly.
  2. Preliminary Review: Supervisors assess the complaint to determine validity and assign IA agents.
  3. Investigation: Agents gather evidence, interview involved parties, and document findings.
  4. Report Compilation: A detailed report is prepared outlining evidence, analysis, and conclusions.
  5. Review and Recommendation: Supervisors review the report and recommend actions or further steps.
  6. Case Closure: Final decisions are documented, and cases are archived securely for future reference.

Types of Misconduct

  • Abuse of authority or power
  • Discrimination, harassment, or hate speech
  • Corruption, bribery, or unethical behavior
  • Failure to follow department policies and procedures
  • Neglect of duty or insubordination
  • Violation of confidentiality or data security

Disciplinary Actions

Disciplinary measures are applied fairly and proportionally based on the severity of misconduct. Potential actions include:

  • Verbal Warning: Informal discussion to correct behavior.
  • Written Reprimand: Formal documentation of misconduct placed in personnel records.
  • Mandatory Training: Additional training or retraining to address deficiencies.
  • Suspension: Temporary removal from duties without pay.
  • Demotion: Reduction in rank or responsibilities.
  • Termination: Permanent removal from the department.

Appeals Process

Personnel subject to disciplinary action have the right to appeal decisions within 14 days of notification. Appeals must be submitted in writing to the IA Supervisor and will be reviewed by a panel for fairness and compliance with department policy.

Training and Development

Internal Affairs personnel are required to participate in ongoing training programs covering investigative techniques, legal updates, ethical standards, and department policies to ensure effective and consistent enforcement.

Confidentiality and Data Security

All IA records, evidence, and reports are confidential and stored securely. Unauthorized disclosure of IA information is subject to disciplinary action. Access is restricted to authorized personnel only.
