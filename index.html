Welcome to the Official Internal Affairs Handbook for Oklahoma RP. This guide outlines protocols, responsibilities, and expectations of IA members to ensure accountability and fairness across the server.

Mission Statement

The Internal Affairs Division upholds integrity by investigating allegations of misconduct, enforcing department standards, and preserving trust within Oklahoma RP.

Roles and Responsibilities

IA Agent

  • Investigate complaints fairly and thoroughly
  • Ensure neutrality and confidentiality
  • Collect and analyze evidence
  • Submit detailed reports to supervisors

IA Supervisor

  • Oversee investigation processes
  • Review and validate agent reports
  • Determine and assign disciplinary actions

Investigation Process

  1. Complaint submitted via ticket system or form
  2. Assigned to an available IA Agent
  3. Evidence and witness statements collected
  4. Agent interviews involved parties
  5. Findings written and submitted to Supervisor
  6. Supervisor reviews and issues a ruling
  7. Case is logged and archived

Step-by-Step: Handling a Deputy Report Ticket

  1. Claim the ticket and introduce yourself in a respectful manner.
  2. Ask for all necessary details, including:
    • Incident summary
    • Date/time of the event
    • Screenshot(s) or clip(s)
    • Usernames or badge numbers
  3. Ask any clarifying questions to ensure accuracy.
  4. Privately reach out to any mentioned parties for interviews.
  5. Document everything in your IA Case Log.
  6. Submit your findings to the IA Supervisor for final judgment.

How to Use the PAWA Bot for Recording in the IA Office

PAWA Bot is used by Internal Affairs to record in-office interviews, interrogations, and formal case discussions. It ensures all proceedings are documented fairly and transparently.

Starting a Recording

  1. Ensure you're in the correct IA Office voice channel.
  2. Type /pawa join – This will summon the PAWA bot to your voice channel.
  3. PAWA will begin recording automatically once it joins.
  4. Announce clearly that the session is being recorded and all parties are present voluntarily.

Stopping a Recording

  1. When the interview or meeting is complete, type /pawa leave.
  2. PAWA will exit the voice channel and finalize the audio file.
  3. The bot will post a download link or notify the IA logging channel (if configured).

Best Practices

  • Do not use PAWA in non-IA voice channels.
  • Ensure that parties being recorded have been informed beforehand.
  • Label all recordings with the case number or complaint ID when saving or logging.
  • Use recordings strictly for official IA business and follow all confidentiality rules.

⚠️ Misuse of the PAWA Bot (e.g. secret recording, using in public voice chats) will result in disciplinary action.

Reporting a Complaint

To file a report, use the official ticket system or complete the IA Complaint Form. Reports should include all available evidence. IA staff will maintain confidentiality and provide status updates as the case progresses.

Types of Misconduct

  • Abuse of power or excessive force
  • Discrimination, harassment, or retaliation
  • Corruption or unethical behavior
  • Neglecting duty or failing to follow policy

Disciplinary Actions

  • Verbal Warning: Informal correction and coaching
  • Written Reprimand: Logged formal warning
  • Retraining: Policy re-education assignment
  • Suspension: Temporary removal from duty
  • Demotion: Rank reduction due to misconduct
  • Termination: Removal from Oklahoma RP departments

Zero Tolerance Policy

Oklahoma RP enforces a strict zero tolerance policy on the following:

  • Intentional misuse of IA powers
  • Harassment, discrimination, or retaliation within the department
  • Case tampering, evidence falsification, or breach of confidentiality
  • Any form of corruption or abuse of internal systems

Any violation will result in immediate suspension pending investigation and possible termination.
