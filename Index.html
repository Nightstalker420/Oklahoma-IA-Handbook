Welcome to the Official Internal Affairs (IA) Handbook for Oklahoma RP. This comprehensive guide provides policies, procedures, and responsibilities to ensure fairness, accountability, and professionalism within our department.

Mission Statement

The mission of the Internal Affairs Division is to uphold honesty, fairness, and integrity across all departmental operations. IA is committed to thorough, impartial investigations and fostering trust within the community and department.

Roles and Responsibilities

IA Agent Responsibilities

  • Conduct impartial investigations of alleged misconduct.
  • Maintain confidentiality of sensitive information.
  • Document evidence and submit thorough reports.
  • Communicate clearly with supervisors and involved parties.
  • Follow all relevant policies and legal standards.

IA Supervisor Responsibilities

  • Assign and manage investigations ensuring timely completion.
  • Review reports for accuracy and recommend disciplinary action.
  • Provide guidance and oversight to IA agents.
  • Coordinate with leadership to update policies as necessary.

Investigation Process

  1. Complaint Receipt: Complaints received are logged into the IA system immediately.
  2. Preliminary Review: Supervisors evaluate complaints for validity and assign IA agents.
  3. Evidence Collection: Gather all relevant evidence including screenshots, logs, and witness statements.
  4. Interviews: Interview involved parties respectfully and document their statements.
  5. Report Compilation: Prepare a detailed report summarizing findings and evidence.
  6. Supervisor Review: Supervisor reviews the report and decides next steps.
  7. Closure and Archiving: Case is closed with outcomes documented; all materials archived securely.

Handling Tickets

The ticket system is critical for managing complaints and reports within the department. Follow these steps for effective ticket handling:

  1. Review the Ticket: Read all provided information carefully. Check for attached evidence such as screenshots, videos, or chat logs.
  2. Verify the Details: Confirm dates, involved members, and the nature of the complaint. Reach out for clarification if needed via private message.
  3. Gather Additional Evidence: If necessary, collect further proof from Discord logs or witnesses.
  4. Conduct Interviews: Schedule voice or text interviews with complainants and subjects to understand both sides.
  5. Document Everything: Record notes from interviews and evidence in the ticket system.
  6. Submit a Report: Write a clear, objective summary of findings and recommendations.
  7. Follow Up: Keep the complainant informed of progress and final outcomes respectfully and confidentially.

Important: Always remain professional, impartial, and maintain confidentiality throughout the ticket handling process.

Types of Misconduct

  • Abuse of authority or power
  • Discrimination, harassment, or hate speech
  • Corruption, bribery, or unethical behavior
  • Failure to follow department policies and procedures
  • Neglect of duty or insubordination
  • Violation of confidentiality or data security

Disciplinary Actions

Disciplinary actions will be taken based on severity and circumstances. These include:

  • Verbal Warning: Informal correction and reminder of expectations.
  • Written Reprimand: Formal documentation placed in personnel file.
  • Mandatory Training: Additional training or counseling to address deficiencies.
  • Suspension: Temporary removal from duties without pay.
  • Demotion: Reduction in rank or responsibilities.
  • Termination: Permanent dismissal from the department.
